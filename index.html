Visualizing Poverty in New York City

New York City uses an alternative poverty measure determined by the Center of Economic Opportunity. This poverty threshold reflects the higher cost of housing in New York City and the importance of non-cash benefits. This visualization can be used to understand what factors determine whether or not a family is in poverty. It is important to remember that this visualization is not a calculator and that all figures are estimates.

What is the poverty threshold in NYC?

The number of adults and children in a household determine that household's poverty level. If a family's income is less than their poverty threshold, they are in poverty.

Section for calculation

The Center for Economic Opportunity calculates whether a household is above or below the poverty threshold using a simple formula.

Wages + Benefits - Costs

Wages

Effects the benefits programs that the family may be eligible for.

Benefits

Income the household receives from benefits programs

Include SNAP (food stamps), WIC (nutrition for women and children), HEAP (heading assistance), and earned income credits (tax refunds).

Costs

Costs that the household incurs.

Include transportation, healthcare, child care, and housing
